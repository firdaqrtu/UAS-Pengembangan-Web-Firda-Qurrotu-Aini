Frequently Asked Questions

Who can use the services of Togawa Library?

Togawa Library is open to the public. Our collections and services are accessible to individuals from all backgrounds, including students, researchers, professionals, and community members.

Do I need a membership to access the library?

Visitors may access reading areas and on-site collections without a membership. However, registration is required to borrow materials, access certain digital resources, and participate in selected programs.

What types of collections are available at Togawa Library?

Togawa Library offers both physical and digital collections, including books, journals, reference materials, electronic databases, and multimedia resources across various disciplines.

Can I access digital resources remotely?

Yes. Registered users may access selected digital resources remotely through the library’s online platforms, subject to licensing and usage policies.

Does the library provide research assistance?

Yes. Professional librarians are available to provide reference and research assistance, including guidance on information searching, source evaluation, and the use of databases.

Are there programs or events organized by the library?

Togawa Library regularly organizes educational programs, workshops, public lectures, and community events aimed at promoting learning and information literacy.

What are the library’s opening hours?

The library is open from Monday to Friday, 08:00 till 18:00, and on Saturdays and Sundays from 08:00 till 16:00. Operating hours may change on public holidays.

How can I contact Togawa Library for further inquiries?

You may contact us by phone, fax, or email as listed on the Contact Us section of the website. Our staff will be happy to assist you with your inquiries.
